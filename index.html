Time Series

Time Series
Intro
Statistical data arranged in chronological i.e. based on time period
Components of time series
Long term movement or Secular Trend
Long term tendency to move upward or downward. Indicates how on the whole, it has behaved over the period under refernce
General tendency to increase decrease or remain constant is called trend of a variable
Sesonal variations
Changes due to rhythmic forces which operate in a regular or periodic manner
various seasons or weather conditions
Methods
Simple Average
Ratio to Trend
Ratio to moving average
Link Relatives
Cyclical variations
Termed as business cycles. Four phases prosperity, recession, depression and recovery
Random or irregular variations
occur on account of random external events
either go very downward or too high upward to attain peeks abruptly
unforeseen, unpredictably forces
wars famines lockouts
Models of Time series
Additive Model
assumed that factors are independent of each other and movements are not affected by one another
Yt = T(Trend) + C(Cyclical) + S(Seasonal) +I(Random)
Multiplicative Model
assumed that forces are interdependent and the overall pattern is the combined result of interaction b/w these forces
Yt = T x C x S x I
Multiplicative Model is generally used
Measurement of Secular Trend
Graphic or Freehand Curve Method
Plot points and draw curve to smoothen out the short run irregularities
Very simple
No maths required
Subjective concept
If length of period is very small that erroneous results will be provided
Method of Semi Averages
Whole time series is classified into two parts and averages for each half is calculated. If odd then middle year is left and two halves are constituted
AM of one half is considered in the mid point so we get two points for two halves, these points are joined to check the overall trend
Method of Moving Averages
It is an average of fixed number of items called periods which moves through a series by dropping the firm item of the previously averages group and adding the next item in each successive average
Called centering and moveing averages are called moving average centred.
Method of Least Squares
